Welcome to the boards, genericbot_.

Just want to remind everyone that experiences and memories should be kept in the dedicated discussion area. With that out of the way, I'd like to add to the topic with what I know.

When Fredbear's opened in '78, it was considered a breakthrough in entertainment and technology. It was even considered a tourist attraction for Hurricane at its peak. Of course, the disappearances of the cofounder and his daughter and the health fiasco that followed were bad but they were still manageable.

The first rebranding brought back the hype and it was so popular that there were talks about opening locations in California and Washington. That never happened, of course. 85MCI caused a backlash and people boycotted the company and restaurants. The second rebranding in '89 was seen as a desperate attempt and 92MCI made it worse. Some say they only got some 12 parties booked during the last six months.
