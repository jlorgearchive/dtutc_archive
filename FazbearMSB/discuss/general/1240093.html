Alright, I've done some digging through the Salt Lake City Public Library and found lots of information on Stanley.

So, like ignition and JBC have said, Stanley was the founder of a tech company based in Salt Lake City, TechVisions Inc., to be precise. It was a company that specialized in healthcare software and was famous for its Meditech Pro program, which lots of regional hospitals used. If you or your family went to a hospital in Utah in the 1970s, your records were probably managed by Meditech. After the acquisition, Stanley focused on Freddy's instead and gave his colleague TechVisions's ownership. The company went out of business in 1998 unfortunately.

I didn't find any information on Emmie on my trip to the library, though. NeonNinja's planning to go there next week, so maybe he'll find more information there.

Made the trip to SLCPL last Saturday. Since Ani talked about Stanley, I figured I should do some research on Emmie. So, here's what I got:

Emmie completed her degree in Communications in '75 and joined a pretty famous Utah-based advertising agency called BrightStar Communications, known for its creative marketing. Her talent showed as she was responsible for some of the more commonly-shown ads in Utah then (she did some of KSL's ads in the late 70s).

I also found that a year before buying Freddy's, Emmie was involved in a groundbreaking campaign for Nike which earned her the prestigious BrightStar Excellence in Advertising Award.

Not exactly pre-Freddy's, but I also found out that Emmie's husband is Cory Minett, who had a brief period working with Sabine Belofsky, a cartoonist who worked on none other than Freddy & Friends: On Tour!
