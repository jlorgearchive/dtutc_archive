Well, where do you want to begin?

On a more serious note, Freddy's was a restaurant that first opened in the summer of 1984, and quickly became very successful. It was originally called... Freddybear's? Something like that. I'm not sure about what it was before '84, so I won't go into that. I couldn't if I wanted to LOL. Back on topic, the restaurant opened in Hurricane, St. George, Salt Lake City and Provo, all locations in Utah.

It was extremely popular before multiple children went missing in the restaurant in Hurricane in 1985, leading to it's closure. Eventually more murders occurred in '92, and then it closed for good. There's a lot more I can't get into, but that's the simplest you can summarize the events of the restaurant.

Hope I helped tho I'm sure I got things wrong LOL.

Welcome to the forum, Mark!

JBC's reply got most of the things correct! Though I'd like to chime in on the discussion too. The “Freddybear's” JBC's referring to is Fredbear's Family Diner, the original restaurant. It was founded by two local engineers William Afton and Henry Emily. They kept the name until '84 when they rebranded as Freddy Fazbear's Pizza. Fredbear's was successful at first but quality deteriorated in the early 80s along with some PR disasters (the co-owner's disappearance) ultimately sunk it.

You can find more info by looking through our post history since I can't really say everything here!
